Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about Smart Saver

What is Smart Saver and how is it different from regular credit life insurance?

+

Smart Saver is a supplementary contract that guarantees premium refunds for your credit life insurance. Unlike traditional credit life insurance where you lose all premiums when your loan ends, Smart Saver allows you to recover 25% to 100% of your premiums at different time intervals while maintaining full insurance coverage.

What are the most common real-world situations where Smart Saver provides the best value?

+

1. Housing Loans (15-30 years): Long-term mortgages where you recover ‚Ç±500K-‚Ç±1M+ in premiums instead of losing them.

2. Business Expansion Loans: Entrepreneurs who want to reinvest recovered premiums back into business growth.

3. Multi-Property Investments: Real estate investors minimizing total insurance costs across multiple loans.

4. Young Professionals: 25-35 year-olds with car/home loans who benefit from decades of premium recovery.

5. OFWs with Family Obligations: Overseas workers recovering premiums to support dependents' education and family needs.

6. Growing Families: Parents wanting loan protection now but needing funds back later for children's education.

Who provides the insurance coverage and who guarantees the refund?

+

Your life insurance coverage is provided by licensed insurance companies through our partner network. The premium refund guarantee is independently funded and provided by R2 Solutions through a separate supplementary contract, subject to terms and conditions.

Can I claim partial refunds and still get more money back later?

+

No, claiming any refund terminates your Smart Saver contract. However, your life insurance policy continues to be in force. You can choose the timing that best suits your financial needs, whether that's 25% after 5 years, 50% after 7 years, 75% after 9 years, or 100% after 10 years.

What happens if I become disabled and can't pay premiums?

+

Smart Saver requires your underlying life insurance to include waiver of premiums benefit. If you become totally and permanently disabled, future premium payments are waived while your insurance coverage continues. You can still claim Smart Saver refunds based on actual premiums you paid before the disability occurred.

Will my bank accept external insurance providers for my loan?

+

This varies by bank and loan type. Our first step is always to confirm with your creditor if they accept external insurance providers. If allowed, we help coordinate with them for specific coverage requirements and beneficiary designations to ensure your loan is properly protected.

How are the refund percentages calculated?

+

Refund percentages are calculated based on actual premiums paid towards your insurance policy. For example, if you paid ‚Ç±12,000 annually for 6 years (‚Ç±72,000 total), a 75% refund would be ‚Ç±54,000. The calculation is based on your actual payment history, not theoretical full-term amounts.

What if I pay off my loan early or want to cancel?

+

If you pay off your loan early, your life insurance policy terms will depend on the insurance provider's conditions. Your Smart Saver contract remains separate - you can still claim refunds based on the time elapsed and actual premiums paid, subject to the contract terms and conditions.

Clients pay premiums directly to their insurance provider, and there are no additional processing fees for claiming your refunds.

Protection mechanisms are detailed in the contract terms and conditions. As a growing company, we're continuously strengthening our financial foundation and exploring additional safeguards for client interests. We recommend reviewing the complete contract terms with our agents.

+

No, the Smart Saver contract is tied to the specific insurance policy arranged through our partner network. Changing insurance providers will void the Smart Saver contract. However, you can claim any eligible refunds before making such changes.

What legal protection do I have with my Smart Saver contract?

+

All Smart Saver contracts are legally binding agreements. Clients can request notarized contracts for additional legal strength. As with any contract, standard legal remedies apply in case of disputes, and we're committed to honoring our contractual obligations.

Are Smart Saver refunds considered taxable income?

+

Smart Saver is designed as a return of capital structure, where refunds represent a return of your own premium payments rather than investment gains. This design may provide favorable tax treatment, though refunds are paid in full amounts as specified in your contract. Tax implications, if any, are the responsibility of the client as R2 Solutions does not provide tax advice. We recommend consulting with a tax professional regarding your specific situation.

Can banks or creditors claim my Smart Saver refunds if I face financial difficulties?

+

No, Smart Saver agreement is a private contract between R2 Solutions and the client. However, we recommend consulting with a legal professional about your specific financial situation and applicable laws regarding asset protection.

Can I get Smart Saver without a loan or credit life insurance requirement?
